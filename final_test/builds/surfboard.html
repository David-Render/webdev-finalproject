Beginner Surfboard Build

This beginner surfboard build guide is perfect for those ready to catch their first waves. Learn what parts make up a board, how to size it properly, where to get it, and the gear you'll want to have alongside it.

Board Parts Overview

  • Deck: The top of the board where you stand. Foam decks offer grip and safety.
  • Rocker: The curve from nose to tail where flatter boards paddle easier, more rocker = more control on steeper waves.
  • Fins: Help with stability. Beginners often use single fins or soft thrusters (3-fin setups).
  • Rails: The edges of the board. The rounded rails help beginners with balance and turning.
  • Tail & Nose: Rounded tails and fuller noses give stability when catching whitewater.

What Size Should You Get?

A good beginner board is usually a foam longboard, 7 to 9 feet long. Size depends on weight and height:

  • Under 140 lbs: 6’6” – 7’6” foam board
  • 140–180 lbs: 7’6” – 8’6” soft top
  • 180+ lbs: 8’6” – 9’6” longboard or high-volume hybrid

Look for at least 60–80 liters of volume for easy paddling and stability.

Should You Rent or Buy?

If you're surfing for a weekend or taking lessons, renting is smart. Shops offer complete setups (board, leash, wetsuit). If you're committing long-term, buying pays off. Look for used foam boards or shop starter packages.

Great Beginner Brands

  • Wavestorm: Super affordable foam boards (available at Costco & online).
  • Catch Surf: Fun, durable soft-tops with cool graphics and good performance.
  • South Bay Board Co.: Entry-level longboards and soft tops for all sizes.

Wetsuit Recommendations

  • 3/2mm full suit: Ideal for 58–68°F water, the most common all-rounder.
  • 4/3mm suit: For cooler water (50–58°F) or longer sessions.
  • Spring suit: Short arm/leg wetsuit for warm tropical surf (above 70°F).

Make sure the wetsuit fits tight but doesn’t restrict movement. Neoprene stretches in water.

Other Essentials

  • Leash: Tethers your board to your ankle, it's a must have for safety.
  • Surf wax or traction pad: Stops you from slipping on the deck.
  • Rash guard: If not wearing a wetsuit, prevents sunburn and skin irritation.
  • Soft roof racks: If transporting the board, grab soft racks or straps.
