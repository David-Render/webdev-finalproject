Beginner Skateboard Build

This beginner skateboard guide is perfect for those ready to start rolling! Learn what makes up a board, how to pick the right size, when to buy complete setups, and the best beginner gear.

Board Parts Overview

  • Deck: The wooden platform you stand on. Beginners benefit from medium width decks (7.75\"–8.0\").
  • Trucks: Metal T-shaped parts connecting wheels to deck. Medium or low trucks are ideal to start.
  • Wheels: Softer wheels (around 92a) make learning smoother, especially on rough sidewalks.
  • Bearings: Let your wheels spin. ABEC 5-7 rated bearings are good for starters.
  • Grip Tape: The sandpaper-like top sheet that keeps your feet in place.

Choosing the Right Size

Board width matters more than length for beginners:

  • Under 5'3\" tall: 7.5\"–7.75\" width deck
  • 5'3\"–5'8\" tall: 7.75\"–8.0\" width deck
  • 5'8\"+ tall: 8.0\"+ width deck (especially for park or vert)

Wider boards = more stability; narrower boards = easier flip tricks.

Should You Buy or Build?

For true beginners, buying a **complete setup** is smart (deck, trucks, wheels all installed). As you progress, you can customize each part.

Good Beginner Brands

  • Enjoi: Affordable complete boards with fun graphics.
  • Santa Cruz: Great beginner decks with classic designs.
  • Powell Peralta: Strong decks and good completes for learning.

Gear Essentials

  • Helmet: Protect your head (CPSC certified).
  • Wrist guards: Highly recommended to prevent fractures on falls.
  • Knee and elbow pads: Important if learning ramps or skateparks.
  • Skate tool: Handy for adjusting trucks and wheels easily.
